messaging

I watched a Ted talk by Derek Sivers where he mentioned why speaking about your goals will make you less likely to talk about it.

In my last blog post, I talked about uninstalling WhatsApp. But in the evening, I had to install it back again for my college project-related conversations. I did the opposite of what I had said.

It is difficult to avoid certain types of technologies that have taken over our lives since the last decade. Google, WhatsApp, and Facebook are among them.

Now that I speak for open-source software, user-privacy and decentralization of the internet, I find big tech companies annoying. It is a monopoly that we ignore, unlike real life. (I am not complaining here. It is alright.)

I had asked Manu about his solution to this problem. He put WhatsApp on his old phone.

I keep Signal on my mobile to talk to my friends. I convinced a few people to install Signal. I don't want to feel the FOMO of being the only one on the platform.

I have been using it for a few days now. I realized that Signal gives a better texting experience since it doesn't have unnecessary features in it.

Unnecessary is a subjective word. For me, it is about making the platform less engaging as a whole so that I can focus on why I am using the platform.
